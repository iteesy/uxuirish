About Me

As an experience designer, I'm excited designing seamless experiences behind complex problems through research and cross-functional collaboration. In tandem, I'm actively practicing to lead a life of curiosity and empathy before judgment.

Growing up, my best friend was the internet and art which led me to study Cognitive Science with a concentration in Human-Computer Interaction at the University of California, Santa Cruz. I thank my uncle everyday for installing Photoshop CS2 on my used IBM desktop because he said I'd like it way better than MS Paint (I was 9).

After hours, I’m comforted by 2000s digicams and slow-burn cinema, but I'll always be creating. Photography, videography, and random bouts of arts & crafts have never left my side.
